Bryce Canyon

Bryce Canyon is one of the many National Parks from Utah. The red rock is carved with naturally forming hoodoo and bridge formations. It has many places to hike and look at the formations from.

Why go to Bryce Canyon?

  • The scenery is spectacular
  • The hikes are good for you
  • Traveling into the park inexpensive
  • The area has good campgrounds to stay at



Bryce Canyon was found by LDS pioneers, named after Ebenezer Bryce. It was found on February 25, 1928, making it nearly 100 years old. Technically, Bryce Canyon is not a canyon because there is no river running through it. While it looks amazing in the summer, Bryce Canyon may look even more stunning in the winter. All the different layers of rock have a thin layer of snow on top, which also breaks up all the red rock. The most famous hoodoo formation is Thor's Hammer, which is a large boulder precariously balanced on a much smaller bobulder.
